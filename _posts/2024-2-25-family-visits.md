**Date**: 2/21/2024   
**Time**: 4pm Eastern   
**Visitors**: Stephen and his Dad

Stephen is a high school sophomore at the Farragut High. His Dad and him came to visit and gave us comments on our Planet+AI program. Although Stephen is not going to participate in our summer course, his comments help us a lot. They were very excited when we showed them one Apollo lunar rock and other meteorites and minerals. They also vistied the LA-ICP-MS Lab at the Department of Earth and Planetary Sciences, and had a pleasant conversation with Dr. Brian Duggan. The Laser Ablation Inductively Coupled Plasma Mass Spectrometry (LA-ICP-MS) is an analytical technique that is used across the Earth, environmental, and planetary sciences. 

In an email, Stephen wrote the following-   
"Having a moon in my hand was exceptional as I could never imagine feeling and witnessing a moon directly in front of me. I knew that space agencies like NASA had access to these moon rocks from Apollo missions but I did not expect there would be moon rocks located in Knoxville. This surreal feeling started having me wonder where other moon rocks would be located across the world and when we were going to have not only moon rocks but possibly rocks from other planets like Mars."
