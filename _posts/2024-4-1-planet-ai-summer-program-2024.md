**When:** June 3-21, 2024   
**Location:** Online   
**Cost:** Free  
**Details:**    

Join our NSF-funded Planet+AI Summer Program!

The University of Tennessee at Knoxville is going to conduct a study our NSF-funded Planet+AI program. We'd like to invite students to participate in this research about their experiences and learning in the program. Participants will share their opinions and feedback to help improve the program. Students do not have to participate if they do not want to. Any decision will not affect participation in the Planet+AI summer program.

To be part of this program:
- Students must be in 9th through 12th grade in the State of TN.
- Students have to be under the age of 18.
- Students have to be willing to participate in the program.
- Students have to be available from June 3-21, 2024.
- Students have to have a computer and have access to Google Gmail and Colab.

The purpose of the research study is to find out what students learn about planetary science, planetary explorations, Artificial intelligence, and their skills and strengths in Planet+AI. Another purpose is to find out how their attitudes toward science change. We want to know what parts of the Planet+AI program help students the most. The Planet+AI curriculum includes a 3-week summer experience. The Leading PI, Ping Wang, will be the overall lead for this summer curriculum. The first week is on planetary science and planetary explorations; and the second week is on math and statistics related; and the third week is on the essentials of Convolutional Neural Networks and computer vision. Ping Wang will be responsible for the first week curriculum. Dr. Vasileios Maroulas, Assistant Vice Chancellor and Deputy Director of AI Tennessee Initiative; Professor of Mathematics, UTK, and his PhD student will be responsible for the second week. Dr. Pengyu Hong, a Professor of Computer Science at Brandeis University, and his PhD student will be responsible for the third week curriculum. During the three weeks, students will use non- ChatGPT AI tools to create artworks and/or digital stories by working directly with code on Google Colab which will not collect students' data. Students will not upload any data directly to any AI tools. The non- ChatGPT AI tools we will use won't have access to any of students' information.

The artworks and/or digital stories students are going to create with non-ChatGPT AI tools will be themed on planetary science and planetary explorations, including Mining on the Moon, From Hawaii to the Moon, Alluvial Fans on Mars, Craters in the Solar System, Searching for Interstellar Objects through Deep Ocean expeditions, Captivating HiRISE Images of Martian Surface, Petrographic Images of the Apollo Lunar Rocks, Meteorites, and other planetary topics.
Students will only use Colab notebooks, as the main Planet+AI AI tools, created and recommended by our instructors, to create artworks and/or digital stories.

Each participating student will be paid for being in this study. Prorated payments will be provided to students who complete the course at a rate of $400/week for 3 weeks, totaling $1,200.

If you have any questions or concerns, you can contact Ping Wang by email at pwang27@utk.edu

This summer program is supported by the National Science Foundation.
